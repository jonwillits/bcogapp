- **2026-07-16** ‚Äî Terminology: **"sensor gain" ‚Üí "connection strength"**, **"base drive" ‚Üí "actuator bias"** (Jon's call). "Gain" is defensible in control terms (a vehicle *is* a proportional controller), but in NN lingo gain means an activation-function slope, not the strength between two units ‚Äî and our parameter is unambiguously a **weight** (`actuator = bias + Œ£(weight √ó sensor)`). Since this same map becomes a network in M4/M5/M7, a vocabulary switch at that seam is expensive; "connection strength" is jargon-free for M1 and maps 1:1 onto "weight" later, and "actuator bias" pairs the creature word with the network word so nothing gets retracted. Renamed in the sim too (`strength`, `bias`, `SensorimotorWeights.bias`). Also fixed a self-inflicted wart: the panel showed **two names for one quantity** (slider "Sensor gain" 2.40 vs readout "Connection strength" ‚àí2.40) ‚Äî the redundant readout is gone, and the slider now displays the *signed* value that appears in the equation while its position still sets magnitude (sign belongs to the phenotype: a 3a with a positive weight just *is* a 2a).
