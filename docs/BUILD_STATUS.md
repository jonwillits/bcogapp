- **2026-07-16** — Visual: the arena boundary is now a **valley**. Vehicles were bouncing off an invisible wall, which undercut the lab — students are asked to explain behavior from mechanism, and the bounce looked arbitrary. `Terrain.tsx` displaces a plane so the ground is flat inside the arena and ramps smoothly up to a rim outside it, and the grid is now sized to the arena exactly, so where it stops *is* where the bounce happens. Shaped with **Chebyshev distance** (`max(|x|,|z|)`), not radial: the sim's bounds are a square (`reflectInBounds` clamps x and z independently), so a round bowl would have put the visible wall in the wrong place and left the bounce looking arbitrary anyway. Light placement is now ignored outside the bounds — otherwise a click on the hillside buried an orb inside it. Rim uses vertex colors (floor → rim), because in a scene this dark slope shading alone doesn't carry the shape: `#1d2a44` and even `#33456b` rendered essentially black under the default ACES tone mapping, and only a markedly lighter `#55689c` reads. (Diagnosed by temporarily flooding the rim red, which proved the geometry was correct and the problem was purely contrast.) Rim tone also kept clear of 2c's slate, since 2c can't steer and lives against the walls.
- **2026-07-16** — Terminology: **"sensor gain" → "connection strength"**, **"base drive" → "actuator bias"** (Jon's call). "Gain" is defensible in control terms (a vehicle *is* a proportional controller), but in NN lingo gain means an activation-function slope, not the strength between two units — and our parameter is unambiguously a **weight** (`actuator = bias + Σ(weight × sensor)`). Since this same map becomes a network in M4/M5/M7, a vocabulary switch at that seam is expensive; "connection strength" is jargon-free for M1 and maps 1:1 onto "weight" later, and "actuator bias" pairs the creature word with the network word so nothing gets retracted. Renamed in the sim too (`strength`, `bias`, `SensorimotorWeights.bias`). Also fixed a self-inflicted wart: the panel showed **two names for one quantity** (slider "Sensor gain" 2.40 vs readout "Connection strength" −2.40) — the redundant readout is gone, and the slider now displays the *signed* value that appears in the equation while its position still sets magnitude (sign belongs to the phenotype: a 3a with a positive weight just *is* a 2a).
- **2026-07-16** — Inspector: **live numbers + per-vehicle tuning**. The wiring diagram now labels each node with its live value (sensors above, actuators below), and a block shows the arithmetic behind each actuator — `A_L = 0.60 + (−2.40 × 0.35) + (−2.40 × 0.60)` / `= −1.66` — built from the *non-zero* weights only, so the equation *is* the wiring (ipsi shows one term; `full` shows two character-identical expressions for A_L and A_R, making "can't steer" readable rather than asserted). Result sits on its own line: inline, the fully-connected case ran past the panel and hid the very number being sought. **Gain and base moved from `world.params` onto the `Vehicle`** — they describe an individual's nervous system, not the world, so individuals can differ; the inspector tunes only the selected creature (hold five fixed, vary one), and learning will later change them per-individual. Only one connection-strength value exists today (all weights = `sign × gain`), so it's one readout, not four link labels; per-link labels become meaningful once plasticity makes weights diverge. **Bug found and fixed while verifying**: `actuators` are only recomputed in `step()`, so retuning while *paused* left a stale total — the panel showed an equation whose terms had updated but whose total hadn't (visibly wrong arithmetic). `retune()` now refreshes actuators from current sensors; locked with a test asserting the actuator equals its own equation. Known cosmetic limit: terms are shown to 2dp, so hand-adding the *displayed* numbers can differ from the total by ≤0.01 (rounding).

Six varieties now = 3 wiring patterns × 2 signs. `Wiring.crossed: boolean` → `Wiring.pattern: 'ipsilateral' | 'contralateral' | 'full'`, since a boolean can't express fully-connected. **The engine already supported this** — the weights were always a full 2×2 matrix and the old presets just zeroed one diagonal, so ipsi/contra are *sparse special cases of `full`*.
